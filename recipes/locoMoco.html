Loco Moco

Description

This amazing Hawaiian comfort food classic is made by topping rice with a fried burger, which is then smothered with a rich, brown gravy and finished with a fried egg.

Seriously, how did it take until 1949 for someone to think of this combination? We all get hangry sometimes, so whether you're a broke student or an older professional who just wishes you were, this cheap, filling bowl of goodness is the perfect solution. Garnish with green onions.

Ingredients

  • 1 (4 ounce) patties ground beef
  • ½ pinch salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • ½ pinch cayenne pepper
For the Gravy:
  • ¾ cup beef stock
  • ½ tablespoon and ½ teaspoon soy sauce
  • ½ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 drops sesame oil, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoons ketchup
  • 1 teaspoons white sugar
  • ½ tablespoon and ½ teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 tablespoon minced green onions, white and light green parts only
  • 1 eggs
  • 1 cups hot cooked white rice

Directions

  1. Season patties with salt, pepper, and cayenne.
  2. Whisk beef stock, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, sesame oil, ketchup, sugar, and cornstarch together in a bowl.
  3. Melt butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook patty in the hot skillet until a browned crust forms on the bottom, about 5 minutes. Flip; toss in green onions and cook the other side until both beef and onions are browned, 4 to 5 minutes more. Remove patty and most of the green onions to a plate.
  4. Pour beef stock mixture into the skillet. Stir, scraping up browned bits from the bottom, until thickened, about 2 minutes. Reduce heat to low and reserve gravy until needed.
  5. Melt remaining butter in another skillet over medium heat. Add eggs to the skillet and fry side-by-side until whites are set and yolks are still runny.
  6. Divide rice between 2 serving bowls. Place patties over the rice, spoon gravy on top, and add the fried eggs.
